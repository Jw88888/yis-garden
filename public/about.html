About this journal

This is a record of one ordinary backyard and the flowers that pass through it: the wisteria that came with the house, the tulips I plant every November and grade every April, the azaleas, the roses, the tree peony, and the squirrel who audits all of it.

I started writing things down because I kept forgetting — when the cherry peaked, which bulbs the squirrels stole, which rose sulked and which one forgave me. The photos are all mine, taken in and around this garden with my phone, usually in slippers.
